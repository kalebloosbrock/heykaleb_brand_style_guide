Paper carries most surfaces. Navy does the heavy structural and text work. Coral is rationed — it earns attention because it's rare. Teal and blush are seasoning.

Accessibility — the rule that governs the brights. Navy and body grey on paper are AAA. Bright coral and teal are not readable as text (coral 2.69, teal 2.81 on paper). So: bright coral is decoration and fills only — never readable text. Teal Ink (#356E6E) carries accent text on paper. On navy, coral reads harsh, so pair with blush, jade teal, or paper — never coral. On fills: paper text on navy, dark-ink text on coral, white text on teal-deep. Full results in §09.

09 — Accessibility

Readable by everyone.

Audited to WCAG 2.1 AA.

Every pairing was contrast-tested. The structural palette is AAA-grade. Coral stays vivid as decoration and fills; Teal Ink carries accent text on paper; and on navy we pair with blush, jade teal, or paper so nothing reads harsh.

Passes — the workhorses
Navy on paper — 10.06 · AAA
Body grey on paper — 9.15 · AAA
Paper on navy (cover, buttons) — 10.06 · AAA
Dark text on coral button (fill) — 5.35 · AA
Blush “thriving” on navy, large — 5.86 · AA
Jade teal “UXR” on navy, large — 3.58 · AA
The rules
Coral → decoration & fills only, never text
Accent text on paper → Teal Ink #356E6E · 5.16
Eyebrows, links, pivot word → Teal Ink
On navy → blush / jade teal / paper, never coral
Pill fill → Teal-deep + white · 4.68
The governing rule: bright coral is decoration and fills only — never readable text. Teal Ink carries accent text on paper. On navy, never coral — pair with blush, jade teal, or paper. When in doubt on any surface, text is navy, body grey, or teal ink.

The paper texture is decorative, low-opacity, and does not meaningfully lower text contrast. Hairline dividers are decorative and exempt — kept light by intent.

Internal · Why we moved off amber

The previous black-and-amber site shared a hue family with a peer in the AI-research-training space whose brand also leads with golden-yellow on black. At glance scale — LinkedIn, conference slides, course cards — the two read as cousins. This system fixes that: coral is the furthest warm hue from that yellow, navy replaces black, and the texture-plus-engraving craft makes the brand unmistakable at any size. Differentiation now lives in things a competitor can't copy — your palette, your hand, your voice, and your tier.
